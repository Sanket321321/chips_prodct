The plant we toured was Frito-Lay's Killingly, CT site. We watched potatoes picked the day before at a Maine farm be delivered, washed, peeled, sliced and immediately cooked into Lay's Potato chips. Soon after, they were bagged and boxed for delivery to our region. Talk about local food! And with our new program, the NuVal™ Nutritional Scoring System, wouldn't you know these same Killingly-produced Lay's score about the same as their baked counterparts? Why? The fats in Lay's® Classic are heart healthy, helping drive the score up AND with a sodium level lower than Baked! Lay's® Classic, its NuVal™ Score isn't driven down as much. Plus, ingredients can be an indicator— traditional Lay's® has three ingredients: potatoes, oil and salt whereas Baked! has eight ingredients. As we visited Frito-Lay®, we learned how they tend to be the movers and shakers of snack food industry practices. For example, when science indicated trans fats (ie: those found in partially hydrogenated oils) actually did the opposite of what scientists thought they would do—decrease risk for heart disease—Frito-Lay switch the type of fat used in their chips to corn oil. This single switch in 2003 subsequently removed tons of trans fats out of the American food supply. Then in 2006, Frito-Lay switched over to sunflower oil. Thereby increasing the amount of heart healthy fats in the American food supply and removing half the saturated fat (the heart disease promoting fat) from their lines.
